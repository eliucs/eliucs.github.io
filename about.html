About Me.

You've probably been wondering, who am I, and what do I do?

My name is Eric Liu, I'm currently a student at the University of Waterloo, majoring in computer science and minoring in combinatorics & optimization.

I'm very passionate about self-teaching new concepts, working on interesting projects, and collaborating in teams. I think that constantly learning and scanning for new developments in the realm of computer science is crucial to keeping up-to-date with current and future technologies.

In my spare time, I work on a multitude of projects. My most recent ones have been:

  • Kasparov, a UCI compatible chess engine built in Java that incorporates bitboards, alpha-beta pruning and quiescence search
  • Wiki Pi, a platform built in Node.js using Wikipedia to generate courses as a low-cost educational resource alternative
  • tf-idf Text Summarizer, a JavaScript library for text summarization using Porter's Stemmer and tf-idf weighting
  • alexaMD, an Amazon Alexa skill for diagnosing medical conditions through voice commands, winner of 2 awards at Hack Princeton

Other times, I work on mini-projects and experiments, which I use to test and keep myself in check with important concepts with algorithms, object-oriented, dynamic and functional programming.

I've worked with many languages and frameworks. I'm fluent in Java, Python, Javascript, HTML, and CSS. I'm familiar with Flask, C/C++, PHP, jQuery, Node, Express, Angular, MongoDB, MySQL, Django and Android Studio.
